// overview

A faithful port of Evan Wallace's classic WebGL Water to Unity 6 / URP. A pool of water runs as a 256² GPU heightfield (compute-shader ping-pong simulation), while the surface is shaded by in-shader analytic ray tracing — reflecting and refracting the tiled pool, a draggable ball, and a sky cubemap per pixel.

Caustics are produced by projecting the water grid onto the floor to measure light focusing, with soft blob and rim shadows. The whole look — refraction, caustics, shadows — comes from ray math and the simulation, with no mesh physics or screen-space tricks.
